My mind thinks of a place: It exists.

2023

Installation



A Rose is a Rose is a Rose.

-Gertrude Stein


Think of a space. Any space. From anywhere. Somewhere.Now, summon it across skies and oceans, against the current of time, and bring it to mind.Consider the vast world we inhabit—and how it may exist, all at once, right here, right now, within your own perception.



My mind thinks of a place: it exists. interrogates the relationship between physical environment and mental projection. Through the construction of a syntexed structure, this work examines how immaterial cognition acquires spatial presence. As the viewer looks at and through the piece, it challenges the presumed fixity of architectural and psychological boundaries.

This work considers how the act of naming imbues form with the weight of image, feeling, and association. Positioning language as a thin space, the work proposes that if language functions as a container for meaning, any element can acquire the specific weight carried by the speaker. Spatial identity is contingent rather than absolute, subjected to the fluctuations of perception.

Ultimately, the work transforms the act of perception into an intimate collaboration between mind and matter, rendering the boundary between observation and imagination permeable.
