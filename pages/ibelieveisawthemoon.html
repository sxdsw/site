I believe I saw the moon disappear.

2026

Electronic Literature


I believe I saw the moon explores how we make meaning through our interaction with memory. At its center is a speculative story about the moon’s disappearance and the quiet narratives that emerge from fleeting, transient moments.

It asks what happens when we return to the past, knowing that every revisit subtly alters what we remember, yet keeps it alive through the simple act of looking back. The work is an exploration of loneliness, nostalgia, and the stories we tell ourselves when we are left with only what remains.

The piece examines how the web functions as an archive—not as a permanent record, but as a space that slowly loses its connections over time. This digital ephemerality is woven into the experience: The web does not preserve by freezing; it preserves by inviting return. Memory survives through the act of seeking, returning, and piecing together what it means to us. The work is not a fixed archive, but a continuous act of remembrance.
